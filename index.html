Changing the future, one thread at a time

In the United States, 30 to 40 percent of food supply is wasted with 80 billion tons of food waste in 2020 alone. When this food is decaying in landfills it emits methane, a potent greenhouse gas. If we were to reduce our food waste by just 20%, we could avoid 18 million tons of greenhouse gas emissions.


We are breaking down food waste into cellulose using acidic hydrolysis. We’ll proceed with using the Viscose Rayon method to turn our purified cellulose into rayon fibres. With the additional substance like wood pulp, our fabric will not only be cheaper, but also sustainable and organic.
